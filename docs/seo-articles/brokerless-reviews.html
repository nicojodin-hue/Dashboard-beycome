What Is Brokerless?

Brokerless is a flat-fee MLS listing service that lets homeowners list their property on the MLS without hiring a traditional listing agent. The company operates directly in Florida and through a network of partner brokers in California, Texas, New York, Pennsylvania, and other states. Pricing starts at $99 for a basic listing.

The concept is the same as other flat-fee MLS providers like Beycome, Houzeo, and Homecoin — you pay a one-time flat fee instead of a 2.5–3% listing agent commission. According to the National Association of Realtors, the average U.S. real estate commission is 5–6% split between listing and buyer agents. A flat-fee MLS service can save sellers tens of thousands of dollars on a typical home sale.

Brokerless has served the for-sale-by-owner (FSBO) market for nearly two decades. The website looks a little old school — and honestly, who doesn’t love old school? — but it’s functional and straightforward, even if it reflects an older design and technology stack compared to newer platforms.

How Brokerless Works

The Brokerless process is straightforward. Here’s what to expect from signup to closing:

  1. Choose a plan on brokerless.com ($99, $149, or $188 depending on photo count and listing duration).
  2. Complete the online listing form — property details, price, description, and photos (limited to 6 on the $99 plan).
  3. Upload required documents — state-specific disclosures, seller property disclosure, and lead-based paint disclosure if the home was built before 1978.
  4. Listing goes live on the MLS — Brokerless’s licensed partner broker submits your listing to the local MLS, which then syndicates it to Zillow, Realtor.com, Trulia, and other major portals.
  5. Handle your own showings and offers — Brokerless doesn’t manage showings unless you pay the $50 ShowingTime add-on. Offers come directly to you via email or phone.
  6. Negotiate and go under contract — you handle all negotiations. Contract forms are state-specific; Brokerless includes the basics.
  7. Close the deal — you select your own title company or closing attorney. Brokerless doesn’t offer integrated title services.

That’s the full loop. It works — but there’s no dashboard, no AI assistance, and no tools for offer management or document storage. You handle the paperwork in email and spreadsheets, the way FSBO sellers did in 2010.

The Real Cost (After Add-Ons)

At first glance, Brokerless looks super affordable at $99. But here’s the catch — the $99 plan includes only 6 photos, a 6-month listing, no ShowingTime, no open house alerts, and no call forwarding. That’s extremely limited.

Most MLS systems allow 25 to 50+ photos, and Zillow research consistently shows that listings with more photos sell faster and for more money. Six photos barely covers a kitchen, living room, and two bedrooms — not enough to give buyers confidence to schedule a showing.

If you want more photos, ShowingTime, open house alerts, and call forwarding — features most FSBO sellers consider essential — you’re looking at $50 per add-on. Here’s what a realistic Brokerless setup actually costs:

  • Base listing (25 photos, 6 months): $149
  • ShowingTime: +$50
  • Open House Alerts: +$50
  • Call Forwarding: +$50

Realistic total: $299 — and that’s still only for a 6-month listing.

For a 12-month listing with all add-ons, you’re looking at $188 + $50 (ShowingTime) + $50 (Call Forwarding) = $288.

Who Brokerless Is Best For (and Who Should Avoid It)

Brokerless works if you:

  • Have sold a home before and know the FSBO process inside out.
  • Only need MLS exposure — not tools, dashboards, or guidance.
  • Are confident your home will sell in under 6 months.
  • Have your own title company or attorney lined up.
  • Are fine managing showings, offers, and paperwork by email and spreadsheet.

Brokerless may not be the right fit if you:

  • Are a first-time FSBO seller who wants guidance along the way.
  • Need more than 6 photos to showcase your home (most sellers do).
  • Want ShowingTime, open houses, and call forwarding without paying extra.
  • Expect your listing to last longer than 6 months.
  • Want integrated title and closing services.
  • Prefer a modern seller dashboard with offer management and AI support.

Is Brokerless Legit?

Yes — Brokerless is a legitimate flat-fee MLS service. They’ve been around since 2006 and provide real MLS exposure that syndicates to major real estate platforms like Zillow, Realtor.com, and Trulia. Listings are submitted through licensed brokers in each state.

We genuinely like the platform. It’s clearly built on older tech, but it does the job — and does it well. That said, the design, functionality, and overall experience feel dated. There’s no seller dashboard, no built-in offer management, no AI tools, and no closing or title services. It’s simple: you pay, your home gets listed, and you handle the rest.

If your goal is just to get on the MLS, Brokerless works. But if you’re looking for a more complete, modern experience — with tools, guidance, and support from listing to closing — it can feel limited compared to newer flat fee MLS platforms.
