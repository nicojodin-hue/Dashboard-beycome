What Is FlatFeeMLS Realty?

Flat Fee MLS Realty is a Florida-based flat-fee MLS listing service operated by licensed broker Stephen Hachey. The company has been in business since 2006 and offers tiered listing packages starting at $99. They primarily serve the Florida market, with limited coverage in a few additional states through partner brokers.

The premise is straightforward: pay a flat fee, get listed on the local MLS, and your property syndicates to Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin, and other major portals. The seller handles everything else — showings, negotiations, paperwork, and closing.

Flat Fee MLS Realty has facilitated a significant number of transactions over nearly two decades. Below, we examine their pricing, marketing claims, user reviews, and how the service compares to Beycome — based entirely on publicly available information.

2. The $499 Coordination Fee and $400 Buyer Fee Not Shown on Their Pricing Page

Flat Fee MLS Realty's pricing page advertises packages starting at $99. However, their FAQ page discloses two additional fees that are not displayed on their pricing page or in their comparison chart:

1. $499 seller coordination fee: According to their FAQ, a $499 closing coordination fee applies unless the seller uses Flat Fee MLS Realty's affiliated title company. If the seller chooses to work with their own title company, this fee is charged at closing. A seller on the $99 Silver plan who uses their own title company would pay $99 + $499 = $598.

2. $400 buyer transaction fee: According to their FAQ, a $400 fee is charged to the buyer at closing. This fee is paid by the buyer, not the seller — but it is part of the transaction and can affect the buyer's willingness to close or their perception of the deal.

Neither of these fees appears on their main pricing page or in their comparison chart.

For comparison, Beycome's $99 plan charges $0 at closing — no coordination fee to the seller, no transaction fee to the buyer, and no requirement to use a specific title company. We offer Beycome Title as an optional closing service starting at $99 in Florida and $199 in Texas, but it is never required — and there is no extra "coordination fee" whether you use Beycome Title or not.

3. Google Rating Badge vs. Actual Rating

Flat Fee MLS Realty's website displays a Google review badge that shows 5 stars. Their actual Google Business Profile, which anyone can check by searching "Flat Fee MLS Realty" on Google, shows a rating of 4.4 out of 5 as of April 2026.
